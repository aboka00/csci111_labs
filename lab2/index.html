Qaraghandy

Central City

Karaganda or Qaraghandy is the fourth most populous city in Kazakhstan, behind Almaty (Alma-Ata), Nur-Sultan and Shymkent.
Population: 497,777 (2020 Estimate); 459,778 (2009 Census results); 436,864 (1999 Census results).
Karaganda is approximately 230 km south-east of Kazakhstan's capital Nur-Sultan (formerly known as Astana). [Source]




Mining

The city was based around mining. In 1857, on the territory of Karaganda, the Ivanovsky mine was built for the extraction of coal.
In 1931, the mining settlement of Karaganda turned into a working settlement, since 1934 - a city.
Karaganda received the city status on February 10, 1934. Nowadays, it is known as a large industrial and cultural center
